What This Shows

The Lorenz curve visualizes how trade is concentrated across products. The X-axis shows the cumulative percentage of products (ranked from smallest to largest). The Y-axis shows the cumulative percentage of total trade value. The shaded area between the curve and the diagonal represents inequality. The calculated Gini coefficient is 0.602 (0 = perfect equality, 1 = maximum inequality).

How to Use It

  • Hover: See exact percentages at any point on the curve
  • Interpret: If 20%% of products account for 80%% of trade, the curve bows deeply
  • Compare: A curve closer to the diagonal means more equal distribution
